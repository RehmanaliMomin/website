---
title: "The Satisfaction We're Sold"
date: 2026-04-15
description: "A short note on how 'not quite enough' became a product, and we keep buying it."
---

That narrative of elusive satisfaction — the sense that contentment is always one upgrade away — isn't just something we're repeatedly told. It's a story we're literally buying into, all the time.

The machinery is good at this. The next phone, the next role, the next round of validation: each is sold as the thing that closes the gap, and each leaves the gap exactly where it was. That's not a bug in the product. The gap *is* the product. A satisfied customer is a churned one.

I don't think the answer is some grand renunciation. It's smaller and more annoying than that: noticing the move while it's happening. Asking, before the purchase or the promotion or the next scroll, whether this actually closes a gap or just relocates it. Most of the time it relocates it.

Wanting things is fine. Being farmed for your wanting is the part worth resisting.
